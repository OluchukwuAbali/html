My Favourite Meal

The name of my favourite meal is Oha soup and Poundo yam

Origin of Oha Soup

Oha soup is a Nigerian delicacy from the South Eastern region, it takes it's name from the leaf used in the preparation of the soup; the oha leaf(Pterocarpus mildraedii). the oha leaf is often also referred to as "ora" in some igbo dialect. This soup is a staple dish in many Nigerian homes and is usually accommpanied by a variety of complementing swallow e.g garri(eba), fufu, semo pounded/poundo yam etc.

Ingredients

S/n Ingredients Measurement
1 Oha leaves A small bunch
2 Uziza leaves A small bunch
3 Cocoyam/Achi 500g/2tbs
4 Beef,Turkey or any preferred protein 1.2kg
5 Dry fish 2 pieces
6 Stock fish 3 pieces
7 Ground crayfish 1-2 tbs
8 Ogiri igbo(local spice) 1-2 tbs
9 Seasoning cubes 4
10 Habenero peppers 4 large bulbs
11 Salt 1 tbs or to taste
12 Palm oil 1/2 cup
13 Onions 1 small bulb
14 water/meat stock
15 Yam 1 small tuber

Direction for Preparation

  1. Clean the stock and dry fish with salt and warm water, rinse the meat and add all to a pot add seasoning and onions and allow to cook till tender. If using cocoyam you can add to the stock.
  2. Once well cooked add palm oil, remove the cocoyam and pound or blend.
  3. Add ground crayfish, pepper, salt and seasoning to taste and allow to cook for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the pounded or blended cocoyam or if using achi, mix the achi powder into a smooth paste before adding, allow to cook for about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the uziza leaves and the the oha leaves after a minute.
  6. Allow to cook for about a minute.
  7. Your oha soup is ready!!!

For the pounded yam

  1. Peel and cut the yam into small squares, an alternative would be to use poundo yam flour.
  2. Boil till soft.
  3. Pound or blend till smooth and moldable.
